Roger Cañas Henao (born 27 March 1990) is a Colombian professional footballer who plays as a defensive midfielder who plays for Shakhter Karagandy.[1]
Career
[edit]Tranzit
[edit]After playing for Tranzit during the first half of 2010, Cañas was close to signing for Serie A side Udinese Calcio during the summer, but following a poor World Cup for Italy, the Italian Football Federation changed the limitations on foreign players, meaning Udinese could only sign one non-eu player that season.[2]
Jagiellonia Białystok
[edit]In August 2011, he was loaned to Jagiellonia Białystok on a one-year deal.[3]
Shakhter Karagandy
[edit]In January 2012, Cañas signed for Kazakhstan Premier League side FC Shakhter Karagandy.[4]
Astana
[edit]In January 2014 Cañas signed for Metalurh Donetsk,[5][6] however Cañas never played for Metalurh Donetsk as he was released by the club at the beginning of February after medical test showed the presence of only one kidney.[7] Cañas then went on to sign a two-year contract with FC Astana on 2 February 2014.[8] On 24 August 2016, Cañas signed a new contract with the club until the end of the 2018 season.[9]
Loan to APOEL
[edit]On 30 January 2017, Cañas moved on loan to the Cypriot side APOEL FC until the end of the 2016–17 season.[10] He made his official debut on 11 February 2017, coming on as a 77th-minute substitute in APOEL's 1–0 away victory against Karmiotissa for the Cypriot First Division.[11] He scored his first goal for APOEL on 20 March 2017, netting the second goal in his team's 2–0 home victory against AEL Limassol for the Cypriot First Division.[12]
Ordabasy
[edit]On 21 July 2017, Cañas signed for Kazakhstan Premier League side Ordabasy .[13]
Shakhtyor Soligorsk
[edit]On 17 February 2018, Shakhtyor Soligorsk announced the signing of Cañas on a one-year contract.[14]
Irtysh Pavlodar
[edit]On 23 January 2019, Irtysh Pavlodar announced the signing of Cañas.[15] On 3 July 2019, Cañas was released by Irtysh Pavlodar.[16]
