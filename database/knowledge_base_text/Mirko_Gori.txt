Mirko Gori (born 4 February 1993) is an Italian footballer who plays as a midfielder for Serie D club Desenzano.
Career
[edit]Gori started his career at Frosinone. He was the member of the under-16–17 mixed team in 2008–09 season,[2] as well as for the under-20 reserve team in 2009–10 season.[3] On 31 August 2010 Gori was signed by Serie A club Parma along with Daniele Abbracciante in temporary deals[4] for €25,000 each.[5] Parma purchased half of the registration rights of Abbracciante but sent Gori back to city of Frosinone after 4 league appearances for Parma's reserve.[6] Gori was a player for Frosinone in Berretti under-19 reserve league in 2011–12 season, due to the first team had relegated from Serie B in 2011 to Lega Pro Prima Divisione, made the reserve ineligible to Primavera reserve league dedicated to Serie A and B clubs. However, the reserve won the national title for the club that season.[7]
Gori was promoted to the first team for 2012–13 Lega Pro Prima Divisione. He made his debut in 2012–13 Coppa Italia against Delta Porto Tolle.[8] Gori was the central midfielder in 4–3–3 formation in the second round of the cup.[9] as well as left central midfielder in the first round of the league.[10] He made 18 starts in the league.
Gori also received call-up to Italy Lega Pro representative teams for the last group match of 2011–13 International Challenge Trophy against Russia,[11] as well as a friendly match against Oman.[12]
On 27 January 2022, he joined Alessandria on loan until the end of the 2021–22 season.[13]
On 9 August 2022, Gori signed a three-year contract with Triestina.[14] Gori's contract with Triestina was terminated by mutual consent on 14 August 2024.[15]
