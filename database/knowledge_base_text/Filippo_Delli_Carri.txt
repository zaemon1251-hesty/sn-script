Filippo Delli Carri (born 3 May 1999) is an Italian professional footballer who plays as a defender for Serie C Group A club Padova.
Club career
[edit]Pescara
[edit]He is the product of the youth teams of Pescara, he played for their Under-19 squad in the 2015–16 and 2016–17 seasons.[1] He made several bench appearances for Pescara's senior team in the 2016–17 Serie A, but did not appear on the field.[2] He made his debut for Pescara as a starter and played full 120 minutes in a 2017–18 Coppa Italia 5–3 extra time victory over Triestina on 6 August 2017.[3]
Juventus
[edit]On 31 August 2017, he joined Juventus.[4] He played for their under-19 squad in the Campionato Primavera 1 in the 2017–18 season, without any call-ups to the senior squad.
Loan to Rieti
[edit]On 1 October 2018, Serie C club Rieti officially announced that Delli Carri joined them on a season-long loan.[5] He made his Serie C debut for them earlier, on 29 September 2018 in a game against Cavese as a last-minute substitute for Thomas Vasiliou.[6]
Juventus U23
[edit]On 1 September 2019, Delli Carri played his first game for Serie C side Juventus U23, the reserve team of Juventus, in a 3–2 defeat against Siena.[7]
Loan to Salernitana
[edit]On 31 August 2021, he joined Serie A side Salernitana on a season-long loan.[8] On 11 December he made his Serie A debut for the club in a 4–0 defeat against Fiorentina.[9]
Como
[edit]On 5 July 2022, Delli Carri signed a two-year contract with Como.[10]
Padova
[edit]On 10 January 2023, Delli Carri was loaned to Padova for the rest of the season.[11] On 28 June 2023, Delli Carri agreed on a three-year contract to return to Padova on a permanent basis.[12]
Personal life
[edit]Delli Carri's father Daniele played in the Serie A for Fiorentina, Siena, Torino, Piacenza and Genoa in the 1990s and 2000s.[5]
