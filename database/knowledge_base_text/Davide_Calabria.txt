Davide Calabria (Italian pronunciation: [ˈdaːvide kaˈlaːbrja]; born 6 December 1996) is an Italian professional footballer who plays as a right-back for, and captains Serie A club AC Milan.
Club career
[edit]Calabria received his first call-up to the senior team ahead of the away game against S.S. Lazio played on 25 January 2015, but remained an unused substitute.[citation needed] The same year on 30 May, he made his Serie A debut in a 3–1 away victory against Atalanta.[3] In the 2019–20 Serie A, Calabria scored his first goal against AS Roma.[4]
With the departure of Alessio Romagnoli at the start of the 2022–23 Serie A, Calabria was promoted to captain of the team.[5] On 14 January 2023, he scored his first goal of the season, scoring in a 2–2 victory against U.S. Lecce.[6]
Calabria played in the 2023 Supercoppa Italiana final four days later, a 3–0 defeat against rivals Inter Milan in Riyadh.[7] On 29 January, during the 2–5 home victory against Sassuolo, he reached 200 appearances for the Rossoneri in all competitions.[8]
International career
[edit]On 9 November 2020, Calabria received his first call-up to the Italian senior squad by Roberto Mancini for three friendly matches against Estonia, Poland, and Bosnia Herzegovina.[9] The same year on 11 November, Calabria debuted against the first opponent in a 4–0 friendly victory against, coming on as a substitute in the 80th minute.[10]
Style of play
[edit]Calabria is not only a right-foot player, but also can play with his left foot. His first control to prepare the play or to start with the ball at speed is delightful. He is decisive in the tackle and gifted with excellent legs.[11]
