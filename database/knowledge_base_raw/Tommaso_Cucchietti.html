Tommaso Cucchietti (born 24 January 1998) is an Italian footballer who plays as a goalkeeper for Serie C Group C club Potenza.

Club career

[edit]

Torino

[edit]

Cucchetti is a youth product of Torino youth team. He made several appearances on the bench for almost the entire 2016–17 season, but he never appear on the field.

Loan to Reggina

[edit]

On 13 July 2017, Cucchietti was loaned to Serie C club Reggina on a season-long loan deal.[1] On 2 September he made his professional debut for Reggina in Serie C in a 1–0 away defeat against Catanzaro.[2] On 23 September he kept his first clean sheet for the team in a 1–0 away win over Fondi.[3] Three weeks later, on 14 October, he kept his second clean sheet for the club in a 2–0 home win over Fidelis Andria and, three more weeks later, on 4 November, he kept his third clean sheet in a 0–0 away draw against Casertana.[4][5] On 26 March 2018, Cucchietti kept his 10th clean sheet of the season for Reggina in a 0–0 away draw against Siracusa.[6] Cucchietti ended his loan to Reggina with 34 appearances, 11 clean sheets and 36 goals conceded.[7]

Loan to Alessandria

[edit]

On 28 July 2018, Cucchietti was signed by Serie C side Alessandria on a season-long loan.[1] One day later, on 29 July, he made his debut for the club in a 1–0 home defeat against Giana Erminio in the first round of Coppa Italia.[8] On 16 September he made his Serie C debut for Alessandria in a 2–1 away win over Juventus U23.[9] Two weeks later, on 30 September, Cucchietti kept his first clean sheet in a 0–0 home draw against Lucchese.[10] One more week later, on 7 October, he kept his second consecutive clean sheet, a 0–0 away draw against Pisa and, ten days later, on 17 October, he kept his third clean sheet, another 0–0 draw against Olbia.[11][12] Cucchietti ended his loan to Alessandria with 32 appearances, 42 goals conceded and 8 clean sheets.[7]

Loan to Südtirol

[edit]

On 26 July 2019, Cuccchietti was loaned to Serie C club Südtirol on a season-long loan deal.[13] One week later, on 4 August, he made his debut for the club in a 4–2 home win over Città di Fasano in the first round of Coppa Italia.[14] On 25 August, Cucchietti made his league debut in a 2–1 away win over Vis Pesaro.[15] On 21 September he kept his first clean sheet for the club in a 1–0 away win against Arzignano Valchiampo.[16] Four days later, on 25 September, he kept his second clean sheet in a 3–0 home win over Fermana and four more days later, on 29 September, his third consecutive in a 2–0 away win over Alma Juventus Fano.[17][18] Cucchietti ended his season-long loan to Südtirol with 24 appearances, 26 goals conceded and 9 clean sheet.[7]

Gubbio

[edit]

On 19 August 2020, Cucchietti joined to Serie C club Gubbio on an undisclosed fee and he signed a 2-year contract.[19][20][21] Five weeks later, on 27 September, he made his debut for the club as a starter in a 2–0 home defeat against Modena.[22] One month later, on 25 October he kept his first clean sheet for the club in a 0–0 home draw over Legnago Salus.[23] On 21 November, Cucchietti kept his second clean sheet for Gubbio in a 0–0 away draw against Perugia and two days later his third in a 1–0 home win over Triestina.[24][25]

Lucchese

[edit]

On 11 August 2021, he signed a two-year contract with Lucchese.[26] He started the 2020–21 season as a back-up to Jacopo Coletta, appearing only once in the first 15 games of the season and allowing 3 goals in that game.

Foggia

[edit]

On 4 August 2023, Cucchietti joined Foggia on a one-year deal.[27]

International career

[edit]

Cucchietti represented Italy from Under-16 to Under-19 level. On 11 March 2014 he made his debut at Under-16 level in a 2–1 away win over Croatia U-16, one month later he kept his first clean sheet at this level in a 3–0 home win over Poland U-16. On 27 August 2014, Cucchietti made his debut and kept his clean sheet at Under-17 level in a 0–0 away draw against Portugal U-17. Cucchetti, with Italy U-17, played also 2 matches in the 2015 UEFA European Under-17 Championship qualification against Slovakia U-17 and Germany U-17. On 16 September 2015 he made his debut at U-18 level in a 3–2 away defeat against Czech Republic U-18. On 11 August 2016, Cucchietti made his debut at U-19 level, as a substitute replacing Mattia Del Favero in a 1–0 away defeat against Croatia U-19.
